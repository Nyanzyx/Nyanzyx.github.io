Bringing the Player to Life

With Ghosts 'n Goblins as the initial inspiration, early iterations struggled — projectiles couldn't travel far and traversal through levels was cumbersome. After revisiting the design, I drew additional inspiration from Hollow Knight and Blasphemous for their movement systems.

Added Movement Mechanics

Dashing (no iframes — usable mid-air for dodging), Wall Jumping (required in specific areas, also an evasion option), and Wall Sliding (eases timing on wall jumps). The goal: give players more options than just run-and-jump to avoid attacks, similar to how Hollow Knight expands on Mario's basic movement.

For the animation system, I used the PaperZD plugin to support sprite sheets — anything living is 2D, anything static is 3D. This changed how animation blueprints and state machines functioned, but I retained Animation Notifications for potion throwing, which was crucial for making the action feel fluid. Traditional locomotion animations were also added to prevent choppy travel.

Damage System

I studied Super Smash Bros and Hollow Knight to understand how they communicate damage to players. The research showed that sprite flickers and hit stops are the most impactful feedback tools.

The system supports three hit types — Stun, Knockback, and Regular — though prototyping kept all damage as Regular. I added a sprite flicker to indicate invulnerability frames post-hit, and a dynamic material color change that fades in and out based on hits. Combined, these achieve clear and readable player feedback.

Enemy Design

I worked on the Base Enemy, the Imp, and the Boss. The base enemy class passes common functionality (health, damage responses, states) to all enemies, designed to be extensible so others could add onto it.

The Imp
A flying enemy that doesn't use the navmesh — instead uses UE's Perception Component to break from its patrol path and chase the player. Fires homing projectiles with a shoot animation that telegraphs downtime.
  • Perception-based patrol-to-chase transition
  • Homing projectile with brief tracking — predictable with movement
  • Montage plays on shoot and reload for player readability
The General (Boss)
A melee-type boss with clearly telegraphed attacks and phased difficulty escalation. Designed to force use of all movement mechanics and potions.
  • Phase 1: Jump attacks + ground spikes to force movement
  • Phase 2: New ranged slam wave — surprise ranged option
  • Phase 3: 15% faster attacks, activates at 10% HP
  • Transition: Boss exits, spawns 5 minions — pacing reset
Design Reflection

In hindsight, the Phase 2 ranged attack was an unintended disruption — players assume a melee boss won't suddenly gain range. A short cutscene or telegraph moment before Phase 2 would introduce the new ability more gracefully rather than blindsiding the player.
