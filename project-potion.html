Bringing the Player to Life

Early iterations struggled with projectile range and level traversal. After revisiting the drawing board, I pulled additional inspiration from Hollow Knight and Blasphemous for movement design.

Added Movement Mechanics

Dashing with no invincibility frames, usable mid-air to dodge floor-based enemies. Wall Jumping required in specific map areas. Wall Sliding to ease the timing of wall jumps. Goal: give players more options than run-and-jump to avoid attacks.

I used the PaperZD plugin to support sprite sheets so anything living is rendered in 2D while anything static is 3D. Retained Animation Notifications for potion throwing to keep the action fluid despite the changed pipeline.

Damage System

Studied Super Smash Bros and Hollow Knight to understand how they communicate damage. Sprite flickers and hit stops are the most impactful feedback tools. The system supports Stun, Knockback, and Regular hit types. A sprite flicker indicates invulnerability frames and a dynamic material color fade reinforces each hit. Both signals together make damage unambiguous.

Enemy Design

I worked on the base enemy class, the Imp, and the boss. The base class passes common functionality to all enemies and was designed to be extensible so others could add onto it cleanly.

The Imp
A flying enemy that skips the navmesh, using the Perception Component to break from patrol and chase the player. Fires homing projectiles with a shoot montage that telegraphs downtime.
  • Perception-based patrol-to-chase transition
  • Homing projectile with brief tracking window
  • Montage plays on shoot and reload for readability
The General (Boss)
Melee-type boss with clearly telegraphed attacks and phased difficulty escalation. Designed to require all movement mechanics and force potion usage.
  • Phase 1: Jump attacks and ground spikes
  • Phase 2: New ranged slam wave
  • Phase 3: 15 percent speed increase at 10 percent HP
  • Transition: 5 minions as pacing reset
Design Reflection

Phase 2's ranged attack was an unintended disruption. Players assume a melee boss stays melee. A short telegraph or cutscene before Phase 2 would have introduced the new ability more gracefully than the current cold switch.
