Ghost-Inspired AI & Stealth System

Design Philosophy

Inspired by Ghost of Yotei and Ghost of Tsushima, the goal of this AI system is to create enemies that are challenging, predictable, and fair. The system is designed to give players readable openings to master their environment.

Under the hood, this is achieved by blending the performance of a custom C++ State Machine with the flexibility of Unreal Engine 5's AI Perception and Behavior Trees.

Core Perception Mechanics

Standard Line of Sight (LOS) often feels binary and unfair in stealth games. To fix this, I added custom perception rules focused on the player experience:

Vision Zones — Forgiving Margins

Suspicion doesn't fill at a flat rate. If a player is caught dead-center in an enemy's vision cone, they are spotted twice as fast. If caught in the periphery, suspicion fills at half speed. This gives players a crucial split-second to realize their mistake and duck back into cover.

Vertical Blind Spots

To support vertical level design like rooftop assassinations, a custom height-limiter prevents enemies from unrealistically spotting players directly above or below them.

Responsive Hiding

A common bug in stealth games is AI staying locked onto a player who just jumped into cover. This system checks against what actor it currently perceives — so if a player drops into tall grass while being watched, the AI instantly breaks visual lock and investigates the last known location instead of "cheating".
