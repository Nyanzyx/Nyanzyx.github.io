Unsanctified

A third-person soulslike action-adventure. Worked within a 23-person team to design and implement the core combat and weapon systems, player movement, and animation pipeline.

PlatformPC
EngineUnreal Engine 5
Duration13 Months
Team Size23 People
Playtime2 Hours
RoleCombat Designer
⚠ Case study content for this project is being expanded. Check back for detailed writeups on each system.

Responsibilities

  • Utilized Animation Notifies and engine tools to set up animation-based attacks for the player
  • Designed and implemented the weapon system and all weapons
  • Designed and implemented all player character movement and animations
  • Iterated on the 3 C's for the player character
  • Refactored the codebase for a more streamlined team environment
  • Collaborated with artists on timing and feel of combat and locomotion animations
  • Designed enemies that fit properly within the combat sandbox

Scale & Scope

Unsanctified was the largest team project in my portfolio — 23 people across 13 months. A key part of my role was not just designing and implementing systems, but refactoring the codebase to be more navigable for a team that size. Clean architecture became as important as the feature work itself.

Collaboration with the art team on animation timing and combat feel was a significant component — getting the hit windows, attack cancels, and locomotion blending to feel correct required close back-and-forth iteration.
