Player Character

With Curse of the Dead Gods as the main reference — also a roguelike — the main differentiating design goal was a focus on ranged gameplay. I built the initial iteration of the player character combat system before handing it off to another designer, while maintaining ownership of the animations and state machines.

4-Way Locomotion

The player's facing direction is cursor-dependent — they can face any direction regardless of movement direction. This required blend space animations to handle sudden directional changes without snapping, giving the character a fluid feel when strafing or kiting enemies.

At this stage in my development, I hadn't yet learned Animation Montages, so I used standard animation states for actions like reloading. However, I did experiment with multiple idle animations that cycle after a timer — a small addition, but it improved the overall player feel.

Enemy Design

This was my first deep dive into AI design. I was interested in GOAP (Goal Oriented Action Planning) but used Unreal's native Behavior Trees due to limited documentation at the time. All enemies went through two major iterations — the second focused on strafing, multiple states, and more aggressive behavior.

Skeleton
A hybrid melee/ranged enemy with a patrol sequence. Range to player determines which mode activates. Slightly clunky between multiple Behavior Trees — used as the foundational template for all other enemy BT layouts.
  • Base for all other enemy BT layouts and AI controllers
  • Used in late levels to force constant repositioning
  • Getting close disarms the ranged advantage
Mage
A ranged-only enemy with a teleport when the player exits range — it never loses the player. Fires dark flame projectiles while strafing during attacks.
  • First enemy with strafing during attacks
  • Forces players out of cover positions
  • Uses Blueprint Interfaces for BT-to-character attack messages
Bomber
Spawns in clusters and runs straight at the player. Becomes an invulnerable bomb on contact — proximity detonation. Color changes to signal when armed.
  • Forces kiting — standing still is fatal
  • Cluster spawning creates priority decisions
  • Color feedback when armed — readable under pressure
Earth Walker
A power enemy — big, slow, high damage. Ground slam triggers a player-targeted AoE stun at range. Designed to force sprint usage to escape.
  • AoE requires sprint to reliably escape
  • Large hitbox — easy to hit, but tanky
  • Repositioning enemy — can't fight it while standing still
