Player Character

With Curse of the Dead Gods as the main reference, the differentiating design goal was ranged-focused gameplay. I built the initial iteration of the combat system before handing it off, while maintaining ownership of the animations and state machines throughout.

4-Way Locomotion

Facing direction is cursor-dependent - the player can face any direction regardless of movement direction. This required blend space animations to handle sudden directional changes without snapping, making strafing and kiting feel fluid rather than robotic.

Enemy Design

My first deep dive into AI design. Used Unreal's native Behavior Trees. All enemies went through two iterations - the second focused on strafing, multiple states, and more aggressive behavior overall.

Skeleton
Hybrid melee and ranged. Range to player determines active mode. Served as the base template for all other enemy BT layouts and AI controllers.
  • Foundation for all other enemy setups
  • Forces constant repositioning in late levels
  • Closing distance disarms the ranged advantage
Mage
Ranged-only. Teleports when the player exits effective range - never loses the player. Strafes while attacking.
  • First enemy with attack strafing
  • Forces players out of cover
  • BP Interfaces for BT-to-character messaging
Bomber
Spawns in clusters and runs straight at the player. Becomes an invulnerable bomb on contact. Color changes to signal when armed.
  • Standing still is fatal - must kite
  • Cluster spawning forces priority decisions
  • Color change provides armed feedback
Earth Walker
Power enemy - big, slow, high damage. Ground slam triggers a player-targeted AoE stun large enough that sprint is required to escape reliably.
  • AoE forces sprint usage to escape
  • Large hitbox - easy to hit but very tanky
  • Cannot be engaged while standing still
