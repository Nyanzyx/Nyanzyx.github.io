Player Movement

With ULTRAKILL as the base reference, player movement and feel are a huge component in allowing players to feel empowered. Two of our design pillars were Push-Forward Combat and Free Form Movement, so I replicated ULTRAKILL's movement as closely as possible with key changes in how certain actions chain together.

The movement suite includes Wall Jumping, Wall Clinging, Sliding, Dashing, Ground Pound, and standard Walking and Jumping. These mechanics are all necessary in different situations — traversal becomes both a defensive and offensive option in combat.

The Parry System

Creating a projectile parry that felt good to players was extremely challenging, particularly when balancing across multiple gameplay aspects. The key early decision was between anticipation-based vs. reaction-based input.

Design Decision: Reaction-Based Parry

We settled on reaction-based — the feedback is instant the moment the player presses the keybind. This lowers the barrier to entry while making the mechanic feel better, since the reward is immediate.

Each projectile required individual tuning. I used polymorphism to pass parry functionality down to child projectile classes — all projectiles inherit the parry code from the parent, so I only needed to tweak numbers and scale sizes per instance.

I also used Blueprint Interfaces to send messages from the player to projectiles. The alternative — casting directly to each projectile class — would have caused performance issues at scale.

Enemy Design

Enemies are the main obstacle for completing levels. I wanted each enemy to feel readable individually, but create dramatically different encounter dynamics when combined. They're designed to appear simple one-on-one, but create cascading pressure in multi-enemy situations.

Architecture: Base Enemy Class

I built a base class holding all shared major functions — Combat Manager (animation-tied attacks and states), Health Component (universal for player and enemies), and State Manager (current state tracking with component-level checks).

The Chaser
A simple path-follower that closes distance and leaps when in range. Low health but becomes a genuine threat in groups of 6+, clumping to overwhelm players who don't manage positioning.
  • Instant kill on headshot — rewards accuracy
  • Slightly faster than the player — forces directional changes
  • Designed to be kited and handled last
  • Verticality is the best counter
Tall Terry
A ranged stationary enemy that maintains LOS at any distance or height. Primary target priority in early levels — forces players to hop from Terry to Terry to clear ranged threats first.
  • Only enemy to be staggered on hit
  • Tap with damage to reset attack and focus elsewhere
  • Useful for level design leading lines
  • Slightly larger health pool than Chasers
Gunter
A flying enemy that fires a minigun-style spread. On death, turns itself into a homing projectile — forcing a dodge or parry decision even after the kill.
  • Large hitbox — easy to hit, but high health compensates
  • No headshot point — can't be cheesed by accuracy
  • Projectiles avoidable at base movement speed
  • Death projectile adds a post-kill skill check
BCSD
Ben Special Class Dover — a ground unit difficulty spike. Predicts player velocity to land ranged hits on fast-moving targets. Adds a melee attack at close range, forcing a weave-in-and-out counter rhythm.
  • Velocity-prediction ranged attack
  • Rest window after each attack — capitalize on it
  • Close range triggers melee — stay mid-range
  • Large health pool — the fight takes commitment
