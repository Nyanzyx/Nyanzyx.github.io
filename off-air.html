Player Movement

With ULTRAKILL as the reference, movement and feel are a core part of the empowerment fantasy. Two design pillars — Push-Forward Combat and Free Form Movement — drove the decision to replicate ULTRAKILL's movement with key changes in how actions chain together.

The full movement suite: Wall Jumping, Wall Clinging, Sliding, Dashing, Ground Pound, and standard Walking and Jumping. Each mechanic serves both a defensive and offensive function during combat.

The Parry System

Creating a projectile parry that felt good required balancing across multiple gameplay axes simultaneously. The first key design decision: anticipation-based vs. reaction-based.

Design Decision — Reaction-Based

Reaction-based won. The feedback is instant the moment the player presses the keybind — lower barrier to entry, better feel, and the reward loop is tight. Anticipation-based required prediction too early, reducing accessibility without meaningful skill depth.

Individual tuning per projectile was handled via polymorphism — all projectiles inherit parry code from the parent class, reducing each new projectile to number-tweaking and size scaling. Blueprint Interfaces handled player-to-projectile communication to avoid expensive casting chains at scale.

Enemy Design

All enemies share a base class with three core components: Combat Manager (animation-tied attacks and states), Health Component (universal across player and enemies), and State Manager (current state tracking). Each enemy is individually readable but creates emergent complexity in multi-enemy encounters.

Chaser
Paths toward the player and leaps when in range. Low health fodder that becomes a real threat in groups — clumps when 6+ are present.
  • Instant kill on headshot — rewards accuracy
  • Slightly faster than the player — forces direction changes
  • Designed to be kited and deprioritized
  • Verticality is the primary counter
Tall Terry
Ranged-only. Maintains LOS at any distance or height, moving only to reacquire. Forces players to clear ranged threats first.
  • Only enemy that staggers on hit
  • Tap with any damage to reset the attack
  • Useful anchor for level design leading lines
  • Larger health pool than Chasers
Gunter
A flying minigun platform. On death, becomes a homing projectile — forcing a dodge or parry even after the kill.
  • Large hitbox offset by high health
  • No headshot point — can't be cheesed
  • Projectiles avoidable at base movement speed
  • Death projectile adds a post-kill skill check
BCSD
Ben Special Class Dover. Predicts player velocity for ranged hits. Adds melee at close range — forces a weave-in-and-out counter rhythm.
  • Velocity-prediction on ranged attack
  • Rest window after each attack — exploit it
  • Close distance triggers melee — stay mid-range
  • Large health pool — the fight takes commitment
